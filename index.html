Call for Papers

Our workshop aims to explore the changing landscape of AI, focusing on pluralism. At the workshop, we want to discuss innovative approaches to responsibly integrate pluralistic perspectives from different people into technological development. Our workshop covers a broad range of topics for the development of pluralistic and inclusive AI, such as disagreement, value pluralism, and algorithmic diversity.

  1. Disagreement: refers to human-human opinions being used to train downstream models, human-AI conflicts in collaboration or decision making, and more to be explored.
  2. Value pluralism: acknowledges the coexistence of multiple ethical and cultural values that could inform the design and deployment of AI technologies.
  3. And more.

We are looking forward to expanding the conversation to include new datasets, algorithms, methods, evaluation, or overarching theories that are used for pluralistic AI development. We also invite a variety of applications and challenging fields, such as crowdsourcing, hate speech, public health, AI safety, ethical considerations, and creativity. We invite broad contributions, from AI development to human-centered interaction and interface design, to practical theories for better model alignment, all grounded in a commitment to embracing human diversity and the complex role of AI in our community and society.

Specific Questions:

  • How to define pluralistic AI? Why is pluralism important in AI development?
  • How to achieve it from an interdisciplinary lens? How do AI researchers collaborate with researchers in other disciplines, such as social science, political science, psychology, and more.
  • How do we implement pluralistic approaches to data collection (pretraining, preference data, etc.)
  • How do we devise pluralistic ML training methods and learning algorithms?
  • How do we design evaluation metrics and datasets for pluralistic ML?
  • How do we assess or measure the social impact of pluralistic AI (e.g., harms or benefits)?
  • How do we deal with pluralistic AI representing values that are offensive to some?
  • How do we design pluralistic AI interfaces for better user experience? And how do we navigate privacy challenges in pluralistic AI?

Submission Instructions

We plan to have accepted non-archival papers hosted on our website and an opt-in archival proceedings of relevant papers. All submissions should be in PDF format and made through OpenReview submission portal. Submissions must be anonymized following NeurIPS guidelines, but a preprint policy will not be enforced.

Authors are invited to submit papers of ??? pages, with unlimited pages for references and appendices. It should follow the NeurIPS template style, which can be found here.

The research presented in these papers should be substantially original. Regardless of their length, all submissions will undergo a single-track review process. All submissions must be anonymous for double-blind review. No author information should be included in the papers, and self-references that identify the authors should be avoided or anonymized. We expect each paper to be reviewed by at least three reviewers.
